Real-scale stress test · 3 mL front panel · long product name

The tightest case. The 3 mL front panel art zone is only ~25.4 mm wide (40% of the 63.5 mm wrap). "PEPTIDE" (7 letters) fits at full size, but a long SKU like TIRZEPATIDE (11 letters) is far wider - so the product name is width-constrained to the panel and never overflows. Below: the exact stack at actual print size, and enlarged for inspection.

Actual size · 25.4 × 30 mm · hold a ruler to the screen
Enlarged for inspection
  • Measured at the full product size (4.8 mm cap): even "PEPTIDE" is ~26.9 mm - already a hair over the 25.4 mm front panel - and "BPC-157" ~26.6 mm. Longer names blow past it: "TIRZEPATIDE" ~41 mm, "RETATRURIDE" ~41 mm - and the widest SKU, "CJC1295/IPAMORELIN", ~67 mm. So the product name cannot sit at full size in a strict 40% panel, even for short names.
  • Verdict - two fixes, use both: (1) let the product name breathe past the strict 40% - it is the hero element and reads face-on even at ~50% of the wrap (~30 mm on the 3 mL), because the glass curves gently. (2) Auto-fit the name to that width: short names (PEPTIDE, BPC-157) sit at full 4.8 mm; longer names (TIRZEPATIDE, CJC1295/IPAMORELIN) step the cap height down to fit. Don't squeeze the letters more than ~10% - below that, drop a size so it stays legible.
  • "RESEARCH GROUP" is the other width risk: on the 3 mL, keep tracking at +0.30em (not +0.35em) so it stays inside the panel.
  • The 2 mL (Ø16) and the tightest names should always get a physical print proof before the run - screen previews can't confirm sub-2 mm legibility.
